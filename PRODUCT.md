# Product

## Register

product

## Users

A mix: the primary builder (personal use), casual concert-goers discovering what's on locally, and dedicated fans who follow specific artists and dig into setlist history. Context varies from "what's happening this weekend?" to "what did they play last tour?" — all within the same session.

## Product Purpose

Live Music Tracker is a fast-lookup tool for upcoming shows and setlist history. It exists to get music fans from "what's playing?" to a show detail page with setlist accordion in as few steps as possible. Success = zero friction between curiosity and information.

## Brand Personality

Fast, focused, no-frills. Utility is the identity. The app should feel like a well-sharpened knife: nothing extra, nothing missing, immediate.

## Anti-references

- **Ticketmaster / StubHub**: corporate, overwhelming, pushy CTAs, ad-heavy — the opposite of this
- **Spotify**: algorithm-first, card grids, over-designed discovery flows, streaming-app energy
- **Generic SaaS dark UI**: Vercel/Linear clones — slate grays + blue accent = every other dark-mode tool (the current palette risks this)

## Design Principles

1. **Speed is the feature**: every interaction must feel instant. Don't decorate what should just work.
2. **Show the signal, hide the noise**: dense information without clutter — what you need on screen, what you don't off it.
3. **Concert venue, not control panel**: the dark theme earns its place by evoking a gig, not a SaaS dashboard.
4. **Utility as identity**: restraint is a design choice, not a failure of imagination. No-frills IS the personality.
5. **Fan-first framing**: the language, hierarchy, and flow should feel like it was built by someone who goes to shows — not a product team building a ticketing widget.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Keyboard navigable. Adequate contrast on all text. Reduced motion respected via `@media (prefers-reduced-motion)`.
